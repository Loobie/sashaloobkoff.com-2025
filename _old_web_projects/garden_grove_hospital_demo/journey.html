We are delighted you have inquired about our program at the Center for Surgical Weight Control at Garden Grove Hospital and we look forward to providing you with excellent care. We have provided a brief outline of the steps you will take on your journey. We encourage you to review the following information and let us know how we can be of service to you as you prepare to make one of the most important life decisions to better your health. We are dedicated to serving your needs so please don’t hesitate to call us should you require additional information.


At the center, we have found that it is helpful to interact with other potential candidates for surgery in a supportive environment so that we can provide detailed information to help you make an educated decision. We provide free seminars every month to give potential patients a chance to gain knowledge about the surgery and to provide an opportunity to ask questions of our surgeon. Some of the topics discussed include anatomy and physiology changes, risks and benefits of obesity surgery and nutrition. Necessary lifestyle changes associated with surgery and the maintenance of weight-loss are also outlined.


Immediately following the lectures, we can arrange a brief, private consultation with our surgeon to give you an opportunity to discuss any specific questions you may have.
1. Patient Information Sheet and Patient History Questionnaire
The questionnaire will provide us with information regarding your medical history including weight, dietary and nutrition history as well as weight-related illnesses. Please take time to complete the enclosed information sheet and questionnaire. If you have questions regarding any portion of these forms, please leave these areas blank. Fax or mail the forms to our office or bring them with you to any one of our community seminars along with a copy (front and back) of your insurance card(s).
2. Review and Processing of Patient Information
A nurse from our office will contact you to review your Patient History Questionnaire and answer any questions you may have. After completion of all necessary forms, a letter of medical necessity will be composed and sent to your insurance carrier(s). A copy of this letter along with a list of insurance requirements needed to process your request will be sent to you.
3. Insurance Authorization
Upon submitting the necessary documentation to our office, we will forward your request to your insurance carrier(s). An insurance analyst will be assigned to you and serves as your “team member” throughout this process. As soon as your insurance carrier(s) notifies us of their decision, we will notify you. Please keep in mind that this notification may take anywhere from one to six weeks. Insurance Authorization does not obligate you to have the surgery.
4. Scheduling the Surgery
Our office will contact you to discuss your benefits, financial responsibility and to give you a schedule of available surgical dates. These dates will be confirmed with you, the hospital and all medical care providers involved with your case.
5. Financial Obligation
Upon receiving insurance authorization for surgery, all expected out-of-pocket expenses will be discussed in detail with you. Patients are responsible for payment of their insurance carrier(s) required co-payment prior to having surgery.


At the Center, we take a comprehensive medical care approach. The following pre-operative patient care services are necessary:

Physician Visits – Each patient must be cleared by an Internist, Psychologist/Psychiatrist and specialists such as a cardiologist, endocrinologist or pulmonologist based on your individual medical history
Physical – A preoperative physical will be scheduled with your surgeon once your surgical date is determined.
Pre-Op Packet – A written confirmation will include dates, financial arrangements and agreements, any necessary pre-operative lab requests and any other details needed to prepare you for surgery.


Post-op care will include frequent visits with your surgeon and nurses during the first year after your surgery. A review of your eating habits, nutrition, and exercise regimen will be conducted on an annual basis. Your follow-up visits will be scheduled after your one-year anniversary. At certain intervals, your blood work will also be reviewed.

The surgeon's professional fees are waived for all follow-up visits however your insurance company will be charged a clinic fee.


You are welcome to attend any of our group meetings, which are held on a monthly basis at the clinic. We encourage our pre-surgical patients to attend at least one session. Our office staff can provide you with a schedule.
