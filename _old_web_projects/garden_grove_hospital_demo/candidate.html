Weight loss surgery may be an option for people who remain severely obese after trying non-surgical approaches, or for people who have an obesity-related disease. Traditional weight loss methods have proven to be unsuccessful for maintaining long-term weight reduction. If you have tried these options with little success, bariatric surgery may be the next step.
A good way to assess your weight is to calculate your Body Mass Index (BMI). Your BMI describes relative weight for height and is significantly correlated with total body fat content. Your health care provider can help you evaluate your BMI and other factors necessary to determine your overall health picture such as family history, level of physical activity, diet, and smoking history.

Weight loss surgery is a serious undertaking, and anyone considering surgery should be well informed about the procedure and its effects. Patients should work with their physician to determine the proper course.
